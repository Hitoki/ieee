Thanks for volunteering some time to take a look at the IEEE Technology Navigator, releasing at the end of February to all IEEE members and the general public! Technology Navigator (TechNav) is a tool that uses engineering and technology concepts to connect you to the rich and ever-evolving base of IEEE content and events.

Most of the information you need is contained in the overview video and the contextual help, so we wonʼt ask you to read much here.

In order to make the most of your time, weʼve asked you to follow 3 steps to using the TechNav and provide your feedback. This should take about 15 minutes.

  1. Login with the user name and password included in your invitation email.
  2. Watch the TechNav overview video.
  3. Pick one or two of the usage examples provided below and follow them from your perspective and personal expertise. In a limited time, this will be more effective than taking a general “look around the tool”.
  4. Complete the survey linked from the green button at the bottom left of the TechNav screen.

Example Use Cases

  • Find a Conference to Attend: navigate through technology concepts and their relationship to each other to ﬁnd a conference that is relevant to your work that you werenʼt speciﬁcally aware of.
  • Discover relevant society/ies to join: Discover one or more society that is related to technology concepts you are interested in, where you are not already a member.
  • Get up to speed on a technology area: ﬁnd a technology concept and related concepts that is new but relevant to you, and review linked resources to gain a general understanding of the current and upcoming research.
  • Explore published work: pick a technology concept relevant to you, and explore standards and XPlore articles related to the concept.

Please use the Support tab at the left side of the Technology Navigator screen to send us any feedback. Thanks again for your time!
